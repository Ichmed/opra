One Page Roleplaying

Basics

There are two roles: Player and DM. A player has control over one Character.

The DM describes environments and Obstacles, and controls all non-player Characters. An Obstacle can be anything that hinders the player's progress towards their goal: a puzzle, a blocked door, a bouncer, etc.

A Player has access to a set of cards (called Features) that can represent anything from an innate trait like "super strength" over equipment and training up to abstract concepts like "mafia connections". Players use their Features to overcome Obstacles or perform other Actions.

Actions

To perform an Action a player draws cards from a shuffeled deck consisting of three types of cards: Success, Danger and Failure.

  • If a Success card is drawn the action succeeds and the obstacle is overcome.
  • If a Danger card is drawn the penalty for a failure increases
  • If a Failure is drawn the danger is increased by one, the action fails and the player receives a penalty according to the danger level
(All cards are then returned to their owners)

To create a deck, Players adds all their relevant Features as either Success or Failure Cards. The DM may force a Player to add a Feature to the deck. The DM adds a number of their own Failure and Danger Cards to represent difficulty and risk.

Difficulty is determined based on the manner in which the Player wants to resolve a situation. "I want to break open the door", "I look for a key", and "I try to convince the janitor to open the door for me" may all have different Difficulties. Actions with Difficulty 0 may still fail due to a failure card added by a Player. Some Actions with Difficulty 0 may still be impossible without a certain Feature.

The owner of the drawn card describes what events occur to perform the action, or why the action failed, based on the card that was drawn. Consequently only Features that could be used to resolve the situation and the DM's generic Failure cards can be added to a deck.

If a Character fails the DM may do any or all of the following:

  • 1+ Prevent them and/or other Characters from attempting the same Action again
  • 2+ Give them a relevant and mostly negative temporary Feature
  • 3+ Enable/Disable one of their features. A temporary Feature is removed instead of disabled
  • 4+ Repeat one of the above
  • 6+ Repeat one of the above or KILL the Character

Overcome

Overcome a simple obstacle like defeating a goon, searching a room or breaking down a door. Difficulty: 0 to 6

Restore|Remove

Heal an Ally, clean your clothes or restore your Reputation Difficulty: 0 to 6 Success: Disabled or re-enable a Feature of a willing character (Temporary Features are removed instead of disabled). Penalties may be applied to one or both of the characters involved

Create

Find a gun, build a Robot or make a new Ally Difficulty: 2 to 6 Success: Create a temporary Feature in your Hand.

Contest

A physical fight, debate or race between Characters.

Both Characters add all relevant cards to the deck. The Character whose card was drawn succeeds/fails based on that card, the other Character does the opposite.

Assist|Disturb

Give somebody a lift, hand them your sword or coach them in retoric

When another character performs an action you may add one relevant Feature as a Success/Failure card, if that card is drawn you describe what happens.

Protect|Condemn

Safe a life or seal their fate Difficulty: 2 to 6

Flip another Character's Success or Failure no matter this actions outcome.

This actions Danger is increased by the Danger of the original Action.

Double|Nothing

A sudden twist of destiny

Shuffle an additional Failure and Danger Card into the remaining deck

Flip your own Failure if you draw two Success Cards without drawing a Failure Card.

Your Danger continues to increase during this.

Progression

Each Character starts with the same amount of Features (the excact amount is up to the group to decide, but 3-5 are reccomended).

After a Session each Player may turn one temporary Feature into a permanent Feature and/or remove one of their disabled permanent Features.
